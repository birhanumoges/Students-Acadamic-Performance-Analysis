⚠ CONTEXT — EDUCATIONAL CRISIS

Only 8.4% of 581,905 students passed the 2025 Grade 12 entrance exam. A 27.7 percentage point mathematics achievement gap exists between Addis Ababa (62.4%) and Afar (34.7%). Over 1,200 schools recorded zero passes. This project directly addresses these systemic failures.

KEY INSIGHT

School Resources account for 68% of predictive power — and resources are directly actionable through budget allocation, unlike demographic characteristics. Once resources and engagement are accounted for, gender, age, and region have negligible independent effect (SHAP < 0.01).

08 — Recommendations

Evidence-Based
Policy Actions

Derived directly from model findings and SHAP analysis — concrete, prioritized, and tied to measurable outcomes.

📦
Equitable Resource Distribution
Prioritize learning materials, ICT tools, and infrastructure for underserved regions. School Resources Score drives 68% of performance variance — this is the highest-leverage intervention available.
High PrioritySomaliAfar
👥
Reduce Teacher-Student Ratios
The Low-performance cluster operates at 50:1 ratio — exceeding MoE's 40:1 maximum. High performers benefit from 34.5:1. Teacher deployment and training programs for high-risk areas are critical.
InfrastructureRural Focus
⚠️
Deploy Early Warning System
The at-risk classifier (AUC 0.916, Recall 0.83) can identify vulnerable students early — before national exams — when interventions are most effective. System-wide deployment is feasible.
ML DeploymentPrevention
📚
Strengthen Engagement Programs
Engagement is cost-effective in resource-constrained settings (17.8% SHAP). Attendance monitoring, structured homework programs, and tutoring initiatives show measurable return on investment.
Cost-EffectiveAll Regions
🌍
Region-Specific Interventions
Inequality is structural, not ability-based. Apply targeted policy to Somali, Afar, and Benishangul-Gumuz. Monitor conflict-affected regions (Amhara, Tigray, Oromia) with dedicated analytics.
PolicyEquity
👨‍👩‍👧
Promote Parental Involvement
Medium-cluster resilience is partly explained by the highest parental involvement (0.48 vs 0.30 in Low). Structured family engagement programs can partially compensate for resource gaps.
CommunitySDG 4
Known Limitations
🔒
Cross-sectional design — Single academic year snapshot; cannot track individual longitudinal progress or establish causal relationships.
📉
Missing variables — Household income, school travel distance, teacher qualifications, psychological factors, and study time were unavailable.
🔬
Kaggle data source — Results must be validated against official ESS administrative data before policy deployment.
📊
Moderate cluster separation — Silhouette Score of 0.174 reflects overlapping student performance patterns. Clusters are heuristic, not deterministic.
